But then I was home with her, dealing with a real, living creature I took under my care, it got me nowhere.

It wasn't that she was badly behaved. She wasn't lunging at people or destroying things. We just couldn't work together. I couldn't reach her.

I had this picture of what we could be together. A real partnership, a dog I could do everything with. And I could feel the critical stages of her development passing by while I fumbled with techniques that felt increasingly hollow.

I couldn't find good guidance anywhere and I literally cried in desperation.

Maybe you've noticed this too

The people with the biggest followings aren't always the ones with the deepest knowledge. The trainers with views are often very good at social media, which isn't the same as being exceptional at dogs.

At the same time, people recommend trainers on Facebook because they like them as a person, because it's a nice atmosphere on the shared walks. Circles of mutual adoration are built, while the impact on the dogs is secondary.

Let's face it, most people have little idea of what they're doing. That's true in every field, every industry. Most people know enough to sound like they know what they're talking about. Far fewer actually do.
